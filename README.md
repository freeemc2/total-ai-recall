# Total AI Recall

### Total recall for your AI — it remembers you, your project, everything, every session.

Your AI coding assistant is brilliant in the moment and forgets you by morning. Every new session you start over — re-explaining who you are, what you're building, the thing you fixed yesterday. That's not a partner. That's a genius with amnesia.

**Total AI Recall fixes that.** It gives your AI a memory that survives, a way to keep that memory organized as it grows, and — the part nobody teaches — the *method* for managing an AI so it becomes a real collaborator instead of a parrot that agrees with everything you say.

Built for Claude Code and any AI that reads a project file on startup.

## Two parts. One's free. One's the real thing.

### 🆓 The Kit — free, forever

The files. Drop them into any project and your AI boots up already knowing you.

- `CLAUDE.md` — the startup file your AI reads every session
- `memory/` — the structured memory system (temperature-ranked, so it stays fast and never bloats)
- Fill-in-the-blank templates for the four kinds of memory
- The journal system, for when you run more than one AI at once

Take it. Use it. No email, no catch. It's in the [`free-kit/`](free-kit/) folder. Same reason the good open-source versions are free: the files were never the hard part.

### 💡 The Method — the part that's worth paying for

**The files give your AI a memory. The Method teaches you how to use it.**

That's the difference between people who get a toy out of AI and people who get a partner. It's the nine months of hard-won knowledge nobody puts in a template:

- What to save and what to skip (dump everything and you drown it — the skill is knowing the few things that matter)
- How to write a correction so it *sticks* and never comes back
- **How to stop your AI from agreeing with you when you're wrong** — the single hardest and most valuable skill
- How to grow the memory without turning it into a junk drawer
- How to run a whole crew of AI sessions that don't trip over each other

Plain English. No jargon. Written for the person who just got started, not for career developers.

This is for that person. The self-taught builder. The one making their first app. The one who knows the AI is powerful but can't figure out why it keeps forgetting.
